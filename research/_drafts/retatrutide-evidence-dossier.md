# Retatrutide Evidence Dossier

**Working title:** *Retatrutide: The Trial Results Are Real. The Online Certainty Is Not.*

**Status:** Evidence dossier — not for publication

## Why this brief

Iris's authenticated BlueSky scan surfaced casual claims about triple-receptor action, “acceptable safety,” informal “microdosing,” and availability. These are leads, not evidence. This dossier separates the claims from the primary human studies, trial registries, and regulator-facing records that can support a public EOS Research brief.

## Editorial thesis

Retatrutide has meaningful randomized human evidence for weight loss and glycaemic improvement in selected adult populations. It remains investigational in the United States. Its trial evidence does not validate informal microdosing, establish long-term safety or cardiovascular benefit, or show that its triple-receptor pharmacology is itself the reason it will outperform other incretin medicines.

## Non-negotiable exclusions

- No dosing advice, titration recommendations, sourcing guidance, or “microdosing” protocol.
- No claim of worldwide non-approval without direct regulator-by-regulator verification.
- No claim that retatrutide is clinically superior to tirzepatide until head-to-head results exist.
- No claim of cardiovascular, kidney, mortality, durable maintenance, muscle-function, adolescent, pregnancy, or long-term safety benefit without direct evidence.
- BlueSky posts are documented as discourse signals only; none become scientific evidence.
